About My Work

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in Industrial Engineering & Management Systems at the University of Central Florida, working at the intersection of human behavior modeling, digital twins, and intelligent transportation systems.

My research designs simulation environments and digital twin frameworks to enhance Air Traffic Management for Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) — while keeping human experience at the center of every system.

Previously, two years as a Data Analyst at Insight Transportation Consulting applied travel demand modeling to real transit projects across LA Metro, Metra Rail, and others. I bring both academic rigor and industry practice to every problem I touch.

Ph.D. from UCF (Civil Engineering, Smart City focus) and M.S. from UNLV (Electrical & Computer Engineering) — I think in systems, feel in stories, and build with data.

🧠 Human Behavior Modeling

Pedestrian trajectory simulation, social interaction dynamics, receding horizon optimization, and generative modeling of movement in shared spaces.

✈️ Advanced Air Mobility

Digital twin environments for UAM/AAM, air-to-ground communication simulation, synthetic trajectory generation for UAS, GPS-denied drone operations.

🏙️ Intelligent Urban Systems

Travel demand modeling, multi-modal transit analysis, GTFS real-time processing, data-driven urban planning for safer, equitable cities.

📊 Data Science & ML

LSTM networks, reinforcement learning, NLP, optimal control theory, and statistical modeling applied to transportation and mobility datasets.
